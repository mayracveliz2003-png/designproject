Swiss Punk

A tightly tuned grid that got punched in the face — Swiss clarity with a DIY sneer.

I ripped up the rulebook, kept the grid and glued it back together with photocopied tape and attitude. Swiss Punk is where crisp type meets collage, measured space meets chaos, and legibility gets gloriously messy.

What makes it 'Swiss' and what makes it 'Punk'?

Swiss (the typographic side)

Swiss design contributes structure: a preference for grid systems, objective typography (sans serif), and clarity of information. It values legibility and pacing.

Punk (the attitude)

Punk brings disruption: a DIY ethic, collage, deliberate 'mistakes', hand-made marks, and an anti-establishment tone. It embraces rawness and immediacy.
