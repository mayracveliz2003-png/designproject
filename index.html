Swiss Punk

A bold-ish fusion of Swiss typographic rigor and punk's DIY attitude — clean grids with an edge.

Swiss Punk blends the clarity and functionalism of the Swiss (International) Typographic Style with punk's raw, rebellious energy: asymmetry, torn textures, experimental typography and visible process. This site gives a concise explanation, a timeline of its development, and highlights key practitioners who helped shape or inspire the movement.

What makes it 'Swiss' and what makes it 'Punk'?

Swiss (the typographic side)

Swiss design contributes structure: a preference for grid systems, objective typography (sans serif), and clarity of information. It values legibility and pacing.

Punk (the attitude)

Punk brings disruption: a DIY ethic, collage, deliberate 'mistakes', hand-made marks, and an anti-establishment tone. It embraces rawness and immediacy.
