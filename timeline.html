Timeline

Key moments and cultural shifts that informed Swiss Punk.

1950s–1960s
1950s

Rise of the International Typographic Style

Swiss designers like Josef Müller‑Brockmann and Armin Hofmann championed functional grids, objective typography, and clarity — the foundation for the "Swiss" part of Swiss Punk.

1970s
1970s

Punk subculture emerges

Across Europe and the UK, a DIY music and visual culture pushed graphic design into rawer, more immediate territory. Zines, photocopied flyers and hand-lettering became staples.

1980s–1990s
1980s

Experimental typography and bridging trends

Some Swiss-trained designers began experimenting — loosening strict grids, combining precise type with collage and found materials. This experimental work is often cited as the bridge toward Swiss Punk sensibilities.

2000s–present
2000s

Revival and contemporary fusion

Contemporary designers remix Swiss clarity with punk's raw textures for editorial, fashion and street-level graphic work. The result: high-contrast layouts with visible process and attitude.
